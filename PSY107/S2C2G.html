Action Potential Process

StageVoltageWhat Happens
Resting State−70 mvNeuron at rest, ready to fire
Stimulation−70 to −55 mvDepolarization begins (inside becomes less negative)
Threshold Reached−55 mvPoint of no return — action potential fires
Action Potential+40 mvSignal travels down axon (all-or-none)
Refractory PeriodReturns to −70Brief recovery; can't fire again yet
All-or-None Principle: Neuron either fires completely or doesn't fire at all. No partial firing — like a gun trigger.

Synapse & Neurotransmission

1️⃣ Action Potential Arrives
Signal reaches axon terminals
2️⃣ Neurotransmitters Released
Chemical messengers cross synaptic cleft
3️⃣ Bind to Receptors
Lock-and-key fit on next neuron's dendrites
4️⃣ Excite or Inhibit
Either increase or decrease firing likelihood
5️⃣ Reuptake
Neurotransmitters reabsorbed to clear synapse

Seven Major Neurotransmitters (ADSNGE)

Acetylcholine (ACh)
Functions: Muscle movement, memory, learning
Too Little: Alzheimer's disease
Too Much: Muscle spasms
Dopamine
Functions: Pleasure, reward, movement
Too Little: Parkinson's disease
Too Much: Schizophrenia
Serotonin
Functions: Mood, sleep, appetite
Too Little: Depression, anxiety
Drug: SSRIs (Prozac, Zoloft)
Norepinephrine (NOR)
Functions: Alertness, arousal, fight-or-flight
Too Little: Depression, fatigue
Too Much: Anxiety, stress
GABA
Functions: INHIBITORY — reduces neural activity, calming
Too Little: Anxiety, seizures
Drug: Alcohol, Valium increase GABA
Glutamate
Functions: EXCITATORY — increases neural activity, learning
Too Little: Psychosis
Too Much: Seizures, neurotoxicity
Endorphins
Functions: Natural painkillers, pleasure, "runner's high"
Type: Neuromodulators (affect other NTs indirectly)
Similar to: Morphine, opiates
Mnemonic ADSNGE: Acetylcholine, Dopamine, Serotonin, Norepinephrine, GABA, Glutamate, Endorphins

Sympathetic vs. Parasympathetic

⚡ Sympathetic
"Fight or Flight"
When: Stress, danger, excitement

Effects:
⬆️ Heart rate increases
⬆️ Blood pressure up
⬆️ Breathing faster
⬆️ Pupils dilate (widen)
⬆️ Sweating increases
⬇️ Digestion slows
⬆️ Adrenaline released

Purpose: Energy for action
🌿 Parasympathetic
"Rest and Digest"
When: Calm, relaxed, safe

Effects:
⬇️ Heart rate decreases
⬇️ Blood pressure down
⬇️ Breathing slower
⬇️ Pupils constrict (narrow)
⬇️ Sweating decreases
⬆️ Digestion increases
Conservation of energy

Purpose: Recovery & maintenance

Brain Structures

Three Main Regions

🔹 Hindbrain
Basic life functions

• Medulla: Breathing, heart rate (CRITICAL)
• Pons: Sleep, arousal
• Cerebellum: Balance, coordination
• Reticular Formation: Arousal, attention
🔸 Midbrain
Connection hub

• Connects hindbrain to forebrain
• Vision and hearing reflexes
• Movement coordination
• Dopamine production
🔺 Forebrain
Higher-order processing

• Thalamus: Sensory relay
• Hypothalamus: Homeostasis, hunger, thirst
• Limbic System: Emotion, memory
• Cerebral Cortex: Thinking, language

Four Lobes of Cerebral Cortex (FPOT)

🔴 Frontal Lobe
Location: Behind forehead

Functions:
• Motor cortex (movement)
• Planning & decision-making
• Personality, impulse control
• Broca's area (speech production)

Damage: Personality changes, impulsivity (Phineas Gage)
🟢 Parietal Lobe
Location: Top-back of brain

Functions:
• Somatosensory cortex (touch, pain, temperature)
• Spatial processing
• Integrating sensory info

Damage: Can't feel touch, spatial neglect
🔵 Occipital Lobe
Location: Very back of head

Functions:
• Visual cortex
• Processes all visual information
• Vision ONLY

Damage: Blindness (even with healthy eyes)
🟡 Temporal Lobe
Location: Sides (near temples/ears)

Functions:
• Auditory cortex (hearing)
• Wernicke's area (language comprehension)
• Memory, face recognition

Damage: Hearing loss, can't understand language
